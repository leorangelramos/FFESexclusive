Teoria e Contexto em Ação: Presente Perfeito (HAVE / HAS + PP)

Veja em ação:
John: "Hi Ana. Have you seen my keys?"
Ana: "No, I haven't seen them. Has Leo finished the report?"
John: "Yes, he has finished."

Usamos o Presente Perfeito para falar de ações do passado que têm um resultado ou importância *agora*, ou para falar de experiências de vida (sem uma data específica).

A fórmula é: Sujeito + HAVE/HAS + Verbo no Particípio Passado (PP).

  • HAVE: Usado com I, You, We, They. (I have eaten. / I've eaten.)
  • HAS: Usado com He, She, It. (She has eaten. / She's eaten.)
  • Particípio Passado (PP): Verbos regulares terminam em -ED (work -> worked). Verbos irregulares têm uma 3ª forma (go -> went -> gone; see -> saw -> seen).

Exemplos Práticos da Estrutura:

Para I / You / We / They:

  • Afirmativa: I have (I've) finished.
  • Negativa: I have not (haven't) finished.
  • Interrogativa: Have I finished?

Para He / She / It:

  • Afirmativa: She has (She's) eaten.
  • Negativa: She has not (hasn't) eaten.
  • Interrogativa: Has she eaten?

Escolha a forma correta:
1. "I ____ seen that movie."

a) have
b) has
c) am

2. "She ____ finished her homework."

a) have
b) has
c) is

3. "____ you ever been to Japan?"

a) Have
b) Has
c) Did

4. "He has not [_____] the book."

a) read (presente)
b) read (particípio)
c) reading

5. "They have [_____] a new car."

a) buy
b) buyed
c) bought

6. "We [_____] (não comemos) yet."

a) have not eaten
b) did not eat
c) are not eating

7. "My brother [_____] his leg."

a) have broken
b) has broken
c) broke

8. "I [_____] my keys. (E não consigo encontrá-las)"

a) lost
b) have lost
c) did lose

9. "____ she (terminou) the report?"

a) Has she finished
b) Did she finish
c) Have she finished

10. "They [_____] here for five years."

a) lived
b) live
c) have lived
